# Synopsis And About

## Short Synopsis

Knucklebones is a dark tactical dice builder where players cross a snakes-and-ladders style roguelike map, placing each roll with care as they fight, trade, climb, fall, and survive toward the end of the board.

## Medium Synopsis

Knucklebones blends board traversal, tactical dice assignment, and roguelike decision-making into short, punishing runs. Each roll becomes a commitment. Dice can be spent to attack, defend, satisfy thresholds, or prepare for what lies ahead, but there is never enough value to cover everything at once. As the path branches and twists, players must decide whether to chase safety, power, or survival.

Along the way, the run evolves. Dice can be upgraded, altered, replaced, or lost. Strange encounters reshape the build. Combat is not about lucky bursts alone, but about reading risk, meeting thresholds, and surviving the consequences when you fall short.

## Expanded About Copy

Knucklebones is built around meaningful scarcity. The player is always working with limited rolls, limited certainty, and limited protection. The tension comes from assignment: where a die goes matters, what it leaves uncovered matters more, and the board itself refuses to stay neutral.

The world is rendered as a tactile, paper-and-parchment journey through hostile encounters, folkloric figures, and uneasy bargains. Snakes drag the player back into danger. Ladders offer relief, but never without implication. Between combat, thresholds, offerings, and pathing choices, the game asks the player to keep building a run while enduring what the board strips away.

Runs are designed to be replayable, legible, and unforgiving. The drama is not in endless scale, but in the steady accumulation of consequences.
